Survival of the Best Fit

A. What was the motivating factor or factors during the game which resulted in the integration of a Machine Learning model to evaluate job candidates through automated resume review?

During the game, the main motivating factor for integrating a machine learning model into the hiring process was the need to handle a large number of job applications quickly and efficiently. The company wanted to improve the speed of resume screening and reduce the manual workload of recruiters. As the number of applicants increased, it became difficult for humans alone to review every resume fairly and consistently.

Another key factor was the desire for consistency in decision-making. A machine learning model can apply the same criteria to all candidates, which may seem more objective than human judgment. Additionally, the organisation aimed to reduce costs and improve overall productivity by automating repetitive tasks such as resume filtering and candidate ranking.

B. What “good” situations existed in the game?

One of the positive aspects of the game was the increased efficiency in the hiring process. The machine learning system was able to process resumes much faster than a human recruiter, which helped in shortlisting candidates quickly. This made the hiring pipeline smoother and reduced delays in decision-making.

Another good situation was the consistency provided by the model. Unlike humans, who may have unconscious biases or varying opinions, the system applied the same rules to all candidates. This created a standardised evaluation process. Additionally, automation allowed recruiters to focus more on higher-level tasks such as interviews and decision-making rather than spending time on initial screening.

C. What “bad” situations existed in the game?

Despite the advantage, the game clearly showed several negative outcomes. The most significant issue was bias in the machine learning model. The system learned from historical hiring data, which already contained biases. As a result, the model began to favor certain types of candidates while unfairly rejecting others, even if they were qualified.

This led to discrimination based on factors such as gender or background. The model reinforced past patterns instead of correcting them, which made the hiring process unfair. Another problem was the lack of transparency. It was difficult to understand why the system made certain decisions, which reduced trust in the process.

Additionally, over-reliance on automation reduced human judgment, which is important in making fair and balanced hiring decisions.

D. How would you advise the business leader about when and how to implement a Machine Learning model to automate or improve the throughput of a hiring process?

I would advise a business leader to implement machine learning in hiring only when it is truly needed, such as when dealing with a large volume of applications. However, it should not completely replace human decision-making.

To ensure fairness and effectiveness, the model should be trained on diverse and unbiased data. regular audits should be conducted to identify and fix any biases in the system. It is also important to maintain transparency by clearly explaining how decisions are made.

A combination of machine learning and human oversight is the best approach. The system can be used to assist in initial screening, but the final decision should involve human review. Continuous monitoring and improvement of the model are essential to ensure that it remains fair, accurate, and aligned with ethical standards.

Conclusion

The game highlights both the benefits and risks of using machine learning in hiring. While it can improve efficiency and consistency, it can also reinforce bias if not carefully managed. Therefore, organisations must use machine learning responsibly by combining technology with human judgment and ethical practices.
