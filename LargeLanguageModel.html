Large Language Model

A. What book did you choose, and why?

I selected Food and Flavour: A gastronomic guide to health and good living from Project Gutenberg. I chose this book because it focuses on food, nutrition, and the importance of flavour in daily life, which is both interesting and relevant to modern health discussions. Additionally, the book provides insights into cultural differences in cooking and eating habits, making it a valuable text to analyze using an LLM.

b. What did you ask the LLM to do, or how did you interact with the LLM using the book you downloaded from Project Gutenberg?

I used microsoft copilot to analyze the downloaded file. I interacyed with LLM by asking multiple questions be better undersatnd the content of the book.

Some of the prompts I used include:
  • Provide a summary of this book.
  • What is the main idea of this book?
  • List key themes discussed in the book.
  • How does the author compare American and European food culture?
  • What recommendations does the author give for improving diet and cooking?

The LLM responded by explaning that the book emphasises the importance of flavour in food and its connection to health and well-being. It highlighted how the author criticises poor food quality in america and compares it to european culinary traditions. The responses also pointed out key themes such as the importance of proper cooking techniques, appreciation of taste, and the cultiral value of gastronomy.

C. Were there any limitations or challenges you faced while using the LLM?

  • The responses were sometimes generalised and did not include a detailed example from the text.
  • The LLM occasionally repeated similar ideas instead of providing deeper insights.
  • When large sections of the text were used, it was harder for the model to capture all specific points accurately

These challenges showed that although LLMs help summarise and understand texts, they may miss deeper context or nuanced arguments presented by the author.

D. Did the LLM correctly provide the response(s) you expected? What type of LLM patterns did you notice which did or did not align to the way a human might have responded to the same interactions?n?

Overall, the LLm provided accurate and helpful responses. It correctly identified the main ideas, such as the importance of flavor, the criticism of american eating habits, and the comparison with european cuisine.

However, I noticed a difference between LLM responses and human responses:

  • The LLM provided structured and neutral answers without personal opinions.
  • It focused more on summerization rather than critical analysis.
  • A human reader might interpret the cultural critique more deeply or provide personal reflections.

This shows that LLMs are effective tools for quickly understanding large texts, but they still lack the depth of human interpretation and critical thinking.
